Illegal Subletting Stops Here

As short-term rental platforms, like Airbnb, continue to rise in popularity, apartments and landlords are faced with the challenge of ensuring that their tenants are not illegally subletting their property to another party. Cities, on the other hand, have to deal with the issue of accelerating gentrification due to the shortage of affordable housing. Both parties are struggling with these issues.

Property managers are increasingly spending time on monitoring short-term rental sites for their property, dealing with tenants that are illegally subletting, and managing the issues that arise from the unwanted guests.
When illegal subletting occurs on your property, it can have several direct and indirect effects. Initially, there is the adminstrative costs of now having property managers and leasing assistants spend time monitoring websites for your property and dealing with tenants that are caught. There is also the cost due to any issues that arise from unwanted guests living on the property: noise complaints, damaged property, theft, etc.
As if that's not enough, the effect extends outside of the front office as well. Having strangers constantly coming and going can cause a decrease in security and make residents feel unsafe. This can lead to a decrease in community, resident retention, and resident satisfaction, while increasing poor online reviews. You work hard to make sure your residents are happy. Don't let a couple tenants ruin that. 
“Airbnb has removed between 7,000 and 13,500 units of housing from New York City’s long-term rental market...By reducing housing supply, Airbnb has increased the median long-term rent in New York City by 1.4%" - McGill University

Cities around the nation are currently struggling with rapid gentrification. Unfortunately, short-term rental sites are only increasing its pace. While sites like Airbnb initially started with the intent for the average home owner to rent their unused room or house while out of town, many people have started to see Airbnb has a major source of profit. Individuals and businesses have started renting properties (homes and apartments), just to then rent it out on short-term rental sites. As this trend increases, the housing supply in major cities is lowered, causing a shortage in affordable housing.
