OUR
MISSION

To help reduce gentrification across the United States by helping apartment complexes maintain the safety and community that they work so hard to develop.

It Started With a Simple Idea

As cities continue to grow and expand, the issue of gentrification has been widely discussed in the media. For a long time we didn't know how to help though. But after reading an article about the effects that short-term rental platforms are having on the housing market, we decided to do something about it. While we don't think that short-term rental sites are inherently bad, we did realize the issues caused by individuals renting out multiple apartment units with the sole intent of flipping them for a profit. Luckily, apartment complexes saw the issue with this too and that gave way for SubletPatrol.

Locally Operated, Nationally Present 

SubletPatrol is operated in Atlanta, Georgia, but our services are easily scalable to any location in the United States, giving us the ability to solve the issue of gentrification anywhere that we are needed.

New York

“Airbnb has removed between 7,000 and 13,500 units of housing from New York City’s long-term rental market.” - McGill University

Los Angeles

“The 7,316 units taken off the rental market by AirBnB is equivalent to seven years of affordable housing construction in Los Angeles” - ShareBetter
